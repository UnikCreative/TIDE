About Us

Transformative Intellectual Dialogue and Education (TIDE) is a Leadership Programme hosted through grand-guide Awan Ayang. It is an educational channel for building people who will in turn build their communities. This is done both with the support and effort of likeminded individuals who have volunteered to be part of this family initiative of nation building.

TIDE enables leaders to emerge in key aspects of society to take on the role of community development. We believe the best assets of a nation are its human resources and we are investing in this most valuable of assets...

Faq

Frequently Asked Questions

How is it consumed?
Our content will be delivered through New Media platforms - Websites and social media, conferences e.t.c.
How do we produce knowledge through the debates?
It is a process of dialectics in which knowledge is constantly challenged. To be more elaborate, it will be this way: 1) Through panel discussions and at the Roundtable any matter of interest can be raised and scrutinized by experts. That scrutiny certainly will be carried out on ideas, proposals, policies, and whitepapers. Those ideas will be criticized and refined for implementation. The endproduct will be actionable knowledge. 2) The word debate in its ordinary meaning is misleading. But when we are talking of debate here, we are referring to the exchange of ideas which could be through any means including but not limited to article and paper publication, seminars, actual debates e.t.c.
What are the practical ways to fill in the leadership?
As you know, it goes without saying that there is a leadership vacuum which needs to be filled. Nigeria for instance finds herself in the current situation due to the lack of quality leadership. Not because there are no people occupying offices but because those occupying such offices have lost the essence of their position which is responsibility for, and accountability to the people. It is an abdication if you like. Thus, TIDE and the Institute are to provide practical leadership by: 1) Constantly engaging the public (its audience) in discussions about the problems of their society and practical ways to solving those problems. 2) Educate the public (it's audience) about government's responsibility to them and about peaceful, democratic ways to demand those entitlements. 3) Training leaders and policy experts in the institute who will be the agents of a rebirth in their spheres of influence. 4) As a think-tank the institute is the go-to source for refined ideas that have gone through a critical debate process. 5) Through development programmes and empowerment schemes. This is not difficult at all and here is how I ran a particular scheme: I discovered that it sometimes takes very little to create very high impact and I set out to test it. In 2020 I set up the scheme called Strategic Partnership Against Covid-19 Epidemic (SPACE). It was all about using my connection to provide skills acquisition for youths for totally free during the lockdown. All I needed to do was to prevail on my contacts who have these skillsets to teach people for free, so they do not stay indoors wasting precious time. I then posted adverts on social media and people signed up. It worked!! For those skills which my friends did not have I went to other tutors and negotiated for a subsidy that I could pay out of my pocket. I made them see reason that this was giving back to society, and it just worked. Not every tutor obliged but some did and indeed we needed just few. For my friends, the mantra in my mind has always been that if you cannot see a reason to serve or give to society freely then we could not be good friends. Incidentally, it helped me to test the kind of friends I have and to measure the net worth of my network and hurray!!! most of my friends were willing and we trained people in areas such as graphics design, shoemaking, fashion design e.t.c. I could not be prouder and happier! What this means in a nutshell is that we can bring such schemes under the management of the Institute, and we can replicate it anywhere in the world. We can sink a borehole or do anything in any village without any politician. We do not always need the help of politicians. It means that we will not just be talking but doing the talk. Who consumes it?
Our target audience is twofold: 1) In the long term: Nigerian youths, the international community and potential partner organization, opinion leaders, pressure groups and participants (trainees) in the institute 2) Short term: National governments (e.g Nigerian governments)
How do we measure achievement?
The variables for success cannot be set on stone but the vision overall is to shift mindset of society in every nation that we are engaged in by at least 60% and by at least 40% in every subregion over the next 10yrs. Taking Nigeria as a baseline, that will be shifting the mindset of Nigerians to our reasoned way of thinking (developmental thinking) to 60% and 40% of the west African subregion in the next 10 years. And importantly, Wider Debates Institute and TIDE should be seen as that driver of progress.
